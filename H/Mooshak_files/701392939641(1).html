Problem H - All trains go to Beijing

Description

You are spending your holidays in China, and you hear about a general strike in Beijing for better working conditions. As you want to see what's going on, you go to the closest train station to buy a ticket for the next train to Beijing. Unfortunately, the person selling the tickets does not speak a word of English, and you forgot your dictionary in the hotel. So, you check where Beijing is on a big electronic map at the train station, but you can only understand the train directions between the cities, as all the rest is written in Chinese, including the names of the cities.

Many people would give up at this point. However, you are though, and decide to use your brain to find your way to Beijing by train. Since Beijing is the main capital, you assume that there is a direct train from every city to Beijing, whereas there should be no train leaving Beijing due to the strike.

Using this reasoning, you look at the map again, and...

Input

The first line indicates the number of cities (n) and number of connections (m) between those cities. The cities are numbered from 0 to n-1. Then, m lines follow. Each line contains two non-negative values: the source and the destination. Other test cases may follow. There are at most 6000 cities and 1900000 connections.

Output

For each test case, your program should check whether your reasoning above is correct. If it is, the program should output the number corresponding to the city of Beijing. Otherwise, it should output "nix".

Example

5 5
0 3
0 4
1 4
2 4
3 4
5 5
0 1
1 0
2 3
2 4
3 0

Example output:

4
nix
