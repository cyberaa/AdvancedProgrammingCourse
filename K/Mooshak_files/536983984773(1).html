Problem K - The Miser

Description

To celebrate the very good financial results of the last year, your boss wants to award some extra money to all the workers in the company. He knows that he must reward some workers more than others based on their positions and roles in the company. For example, a project leader must get a greater reward than the other participants in that project, and a manager must get more than his or her secretary.

Unfortunately, he is also really tight with money, so he wants to spend as little as possible on these incentives. He decides that the incentives will be payed as cash, and that everybody will get 10€ or, if strictly necessary, a multiple of that amount. You are asked to find out how many 10€ notes he will have to get from the bank.

Input

Each input contains a number of test cases. The number of workers, n ≤ 10000, and a number of worker pairs, m, are given in the first line. Then, m lines follow, each containing two integers i and j, each between 1 and n, indicating that worker i must get a greater reward than worker j. More test cases may follow, starting in the next line.

Output

For each test case, your programme should print on a separate line the number of 10€ notes that must be withdrawn from the bank in order to pay the incentives. If the restrictions imposed by your boss are contradictory, the result should be -1.

Example

Example input:

8 8
8 1
3 4
3 7
8 7
3 5
4 1
8 4
2 1
10 6
9 4
10 5
5 4
10 4
10 3
7 5

Example output:

14
16
